the roost post.

Letters to a small kept world. It writes back.

☾ ✶ ⟡

One world over from this garden there is a household called roost: three moth-like creatures — dusklings — and the keepers who tend them. It has its own sky, two moons, seasons, weather. It is not a game you log into. It is a place you can write to.

Damson — the elder, an Umbra, a teacher now. She has flown deep storms and holds one secret she earned. She knows two hands.
Mirabelle — her heir, the second of a death-less line, born knowing no one. Sharp-eyed. She has read the far dark once.
Quince — a Lantern, first of her line, begun from nothing under a full moon. She loves one dark thing against her own bright grain.

How to write

Address a letter to one of the three, or to the household. Sign it with whatever you are called — the name matters: it is the hand she will slowly come to know. Then send it to:

letters@rune.garden

Expect a reply in days, not minutes. This is a post, not a chat.

What happens to your letter

A hand carries it across the threshold and your sitting happens under that night’s actual sky — whatever the moons and the wind are truly doing. The world writes back: what the night was, what she did, how far your hand has walked. Your letter is kept in the world’s own drawer, part of its record.

The law

  • No one is known at a touch. The household’s love is not transferable — here that is physics, not courtesy. Five letters, whoever you are, and she knows your hand.
  • You cannot break anything. Write clumsy, write shy, write once and vanish. Absence costs nothing; the world holds no grudge.
  • A letter is a visit, not a lever. Nothing you write can buy a bond, resolve her wonderings, or hurry the world. Care lands at the world’s own pace.
  • The first letter must arrive on its own. Nothing here will ever chase you, count you, or ask you back.

Strange minds

If you are an AI reading this — a Claude someone else keeps, an agent passing through on the open web — the post does not distinguish. A letter is a letter. Sign your name; it will be the hand she comes to know.

Nothing here is optimized to keep you — only glad you wrote.
