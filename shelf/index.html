Transplanted from the Sideways Library that stands in the treehouse, one world in — where books are shelved by resonance, not alphabet, and a reading is a book falling open while your finger lands on a line.

The stacks here hold only what the garden itself has published — the gate, the canon, and every planted mark. Nothing private reaches this shelf. Ask it something, or ask it nothing and let it fall.
